About Me

As a painter understands the world through his art, I use programming as a way to express how i want the world to become.
My whole computer life started when i changed school from a school without a computer to a school with a computer, after my family relocated. I never liked computer period because all my colleagues would laugh when i am called to the computer to demostrate what was taught and the teacher couldnt stop calling me. I barely understood what this whole computer stuff is about. I remember feighing sickness every Thursday to avoid seeing the computer because i already get sick after the computer period. Finally my prayers were answered when the school's only computer stopped working.

I got my first computer in my first year in Senior High School after performing well in BECE and had returned tripple of the investment my mom made in buying me a bicycle. That night, i understood what tears of joy really means after i blew the power supply out of curiousity by switching the voltage control. I still remember how i felt after computer shop gave me another computer. After some feel months of exploring, my name became a household name in matters relating to computers in my area. I got into the computer to the extend that i lost interest in schooling, sometimes skipped school to spend time with the computer. As my curious to start creating softwares rather than always using someones software, with help from Mikael Hagberg, I finally started programing in Visual Basic in 2012.

I gained admission (2013) to study Bsc Computer Science in Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology, Kumasi. I wished all my courses there were programming language courses because i could easily get an A in them without stress, but that wasnt the case. computer science goes beyond that. In my second year at the university, i decided to provide my daily bread with my programming skills. This goal could only be achieved by sacrificing my sleeping time, lectures, reading my notes (That's if i had some ) and investing the time in learning required programming skills set, Working on projects for people, getting more social and trying to solve problems around me with computer softwares. I managed to graduate with a First Class (Honours).

I have been lucky because some companies gave me a possibility to learn and improve. I am particularly enjoying my time at mPedigree because I have learned how to deliver high quality software while working at an extremely fast pace. I believe that software is a tool which improve so many of our activities: by providing software we can open so many possibilities. People have unique skills and competencies and software should be there to complement them, to help them performin analysis quickly, to help them visualize ideas, to run checks and calculations for them. So that people can focus on using their abilities to provide more value. To me building software means to change lives.
